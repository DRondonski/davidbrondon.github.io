Overview

This work was part of a VIP research program centered on airfoil geometry generation, aerodynamic performance evaluation, and iterative design improvement. My role focused heavily on the computational pipeline that turns design variables into usable geometry and downstream analysis.

My Contribution

I led development on the Python side of the workflow, helping create a system that:

  • Accepts a compact set of NACA-style input parameters
  • Generates the corresponding airfoil profile automatically
  • Produces geometry suitable for downstream simulation or fabrication
  • Supports rapid iteration for optimization and comparison studies

Research Structure

The broader research effort combined four connected parts:

  • Parametric geometry generation
  • Optimization and search logic
  • CFD-based aerodynamic evaluation
  • Wind tunnel testing for physical validation

Outcome

The result was a cleaner path from input parameters to geometry to analysis, which supported a much more iterative research process and gave the team a practical foundation for future optimization studies.
